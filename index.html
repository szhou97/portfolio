About Me

I spent my early childhood in Shanghai, China before my family moved to the United States in 2013 where I started attending high school. During the time I was involved in the Math team, played trombone in multiple bands, and trained hurdles on the varsity track team.

I attended University of Wisconsin – Madison for my undergraduate degree. At first, my curiosity of how the universe works helped me decide to be a physics and astronomy double major, and during my major courses I had the chance to use Python and MATLAB for writing research papers. That was when I developed a passion for Computer Science and decided to add one more major to my curriculum.

After graduating, I decided to attend Boston University to obtain a master’s degree in computer science to focus and solidify my knowledge and skills in Computer Science. Apart from core courses, my studies had a focus on Computer Systems and Machine Learning.

I had the opportunity to work as a back-end software engineer after graduating where I worked with deploying projects written in Python onto GCP connective warehouse.

Fast forward to today, I am part of the Fasttrack program at Cook Systems working to become a full-stack developer.

When I am not writing codes, I have many hobbies to spend my spare time. Being an active person, I involve in many sports including basketball, snowboarding and bodybuilding. I also find time to play instruments including piano, trombone and guitar and occasionally edit music on FL Studio for my local community event.
